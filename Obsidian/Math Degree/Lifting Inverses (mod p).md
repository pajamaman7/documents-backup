Let $x \in\mathbb{Z}_p$ and suppose $\exists z\in\mathbb{Z}_p:\, zx\equiv 1\pmod{p}$. Then for any [[Modular Arithmetic|modulus]] $p^{e}$ where $\alpha \in\mathbb{Z}$, $x$ has an inverse $\pmod{p^{\alpha}}$. In other words;
$$GCD(x,p)=1\iff GCD(x,p^{\alpha})=1$$
## Doubling Exponents
Let $xy\equiv{1}\pmod{p^{e}}$ and $k$ be such that $xy=1+kp^{e}$. Define $z=y-ykp^{e}$. Then $xz\equiv 1 \pmod{ p^{2e}}$.
